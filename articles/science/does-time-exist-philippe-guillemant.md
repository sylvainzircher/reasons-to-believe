# A Theory of Time, Synchronicity, and the Multiverse Future

## Overview

This is a translated summary of a French-language interview with a researcher (an engineer by training) who has spent over 25 years developing a theory of time intended to explain the phenomenon of synchronicity. He has published this theory in multiple books and academic articles over roughly 15 years. The interview covers the physics foundations of the theory, its philosophical implications, and its practical consequences for how individuals might relate to intention, free will, and personal development.

---

## 1. The Starting Point — Synchronicity and Time

The speaker's central question since adolescence has been: **how can synchronicities be explained?** A synchronicity is a meaningful coincidence — an event that seems to occur with a significance that pure chance cannot satisfactorily account for.

His answer came through engaging with the results of modern physics — **general relativity** and **quantum mechanics** — and concluding that a coherent interpretation of these theories requires accepting two propositions:

- The **future is already realised** (it already exists in some sense).
- The **past still exists** (it has not simply ceased to be).

In standard physics, what we call "the present" has no fundamental status — it does not exist as a privileged moment in the equations. The present is, in the speaker's view, an illusion.

---

## 2. The Core Model — A Pre-Existing Multiverse of Futures

The speaker frames the future not as something being created moment by moment as time passes, but as a **territory that already exists** — like the path ahead of a hiker in a forest. You do not believe the path is being created in real time as you walk; it was already there. The error of ordinary temporal intuition is to think the future is being generated as we move through it.

However, the future is not a single fixed trajectory. It exists in **multiple variants** — a multiverse of possible futures — much as a hiking region contains many possible routes depending on weather, intention, and circumstance.

### The GPS Analogy

For causality to remain coherent, there must be **a specific future toward which we are actually headed** at any given moment — not just an open set of possibilities. The speaker uses the image of a GPS route: there must be a plotted course, or the causal structure of events would break down. But this route is not fixed permanently — it can be **modified**.

True evolution, in his model, does not happen **within** time. It happens **outside of time** through the progressive modification of the GPS route leading us toward our future.

### The Tunnel and the Ocean

For the collective scale, the speaker offers a complementary metaphor:

- Each person (and collectively, humanity) moves through an **invisible tunnel** that orients their steps and destiny throughout life.
- This tunnel **can move** — meaning our future can change.
- The tunnel floats on a **quantum ocean** — the vacuum — which is not empty at all but full of infinitesimal vibrations down to the **Planck length** (~10⁻³⁴ metres).
- It is the **waves in this quantum ocean** that cause the tunnel to shift.

---

## 3. The Role of the Quantum Vacuum and Infinitesimal Vibrations

A central claim of the theory is that **infinitesimally small vibrations** in the vacuum — at the scale of the Planck length — are capable of influencing the course of events. The speaker argues:

- An infinitesimally small piece of information is **as powerful** as a macroscopic piece of information in determining the course of events.
- The difference is that microscopic information takes **time to deploy its effects** — a point the speaker claims is poorly understood in physics.
- This connects to **chaos theory**, but with an important extension: while chaos theory is typically applied only to systems with specific instability properties, the speaker argues that **chaos is present everywhere in nature**, at every interaction between particles or objects.

The mechanism he proposes: at every interaction, there is what he calls a **loss of information** that needs to be compensated. This loss of information:

1. Requires additional dimensions to be added to space-time.
2. Explains why the quantum vacuum acts constantly on our reality.

This influence is long-term: in the short term, standard causality and determinism continue to govern events. It is only over longer time scales that the quantum vacuum vibrations alter the trajectory of what we are going to live.

---

## 4. The Inverse Butterfly Effect — Resistance of the Future

The speaker explicitly addresses the **butterfly effect** (from chaos theory), agreeing it captures something real but arguing it has been **overstated and misunderstood**:

- If the butterfly effect were fully real as commonly described, a pre-existing future that fluctuates would be impossible — the slightest modification would cause a collapse of space-time itself.
- Instead, the speaker claims to have demonstrated an **inverse butterfly effect**: the future has **resistance** — a tendency to reconstitute itself even when the present is disturbed.
- A butterfly **never actually creates a cyclone** on the other side of the planet because a **stabilising phenomenon** prevents it.

The future is therefore not fragile and chaotic but **resilient and self-reconstituting**. Small perturbations do not cascade without limit; the overall trajectory tends to restore itself.

---

## 5. Retrocausality — The Future Acting on the Present

A key element of the theory is **retrocausality**: the future can exert causal influence on the present, not only the other way around.

The mathematical equations of physics are, he argues, **time-symmetric** — causality works in both directions. The "arrow of time" we experience is an illusion arising from a misunderstanding of information in physics.

In practical terms:

- When the future changes (in the "higher" or eternal time he describes), the GPS route is modified.
- This modification then produces **effects in our present** that steer us toward the new future.
- The change in the future therefore **precedes** the changes we notice in the present — in a different dimension of time than the ordinary time we experience.

He distinguishes two layers of time:

- **Cybernetic time** — the ordinary flow of time we experience.
- **Eternal time** (or similar concepts) — the higher temporal dimension in which the future can be modified and retrocausality operates.

---

## 6. The Eternal Present vs. the Lived Present

The speaker distinguishes between:

- **The felt present** — the moment as we subjectively experience it.
- **The eternal present** — the totality of all past and future potentials; the full landscape of what has existed and what could be lived.

We exist within this eternal present, and our **intention** is what navigates us through it toward specific futures. The reason we do not consciously perceive the future is that our intention acts at the **unconscious level** — but it acts nonetheless.

The practical implication: it is fundamental to **cultivate and clarify one's deep intention** — to ensure that what orients us toward our future is genuinely our own, genuinely connected to our life's meaning and to who we truly are.

---

## 7. Synchronicities as Evidence and Navigation Tools

Synchronicities, in this framework, are not random coincidences. They are moments where the **underlying structure of reality** — the pre-existing, shifting multiverse of futures — briefly becomes visible or perceptible to consciousness.

The speaker suggests that:

- Synchronicities should be **paid attention to** because they signal something about one's actual trajectory.
- They do not need to be "provoked" — they are natural phenomena. But deliberately attempting to provoke them is useful as an **experiment**, to verify that reality does not work the way ordinary intuition assumes.
- Being attentive to synchronicities requires first **reconnecting with oneself**.

---

## 8. The Multidimensional Self and the Conditions for Change

The speaker argues that we live as a small, surface-level expression of who we really are. We mistake the appearance of our daily lives for the totality of our existence — when in fact:

- Our lives existed before our birth.
- They will continue after our death.
- We are temporarily inhabiting a particular manifestation of a far larger being.

To genuinely build one's future, one must not look to the **small self** (the ego, ordinary daily life) but to **what we fundamentally are** — the deeper, multidimensional reality of the self.

### Three Forms of Deconditioning

Accessing this deeper self requires **deconditioning** from three sources of conditioning, each progressively harder than the last:

1. **Mental deconditioning** — letting go (lâcher-prise); releasing fixed mental patterns and certainties.
2. **Emotional deconditioning** — learning to trust oneself; releasing conditioned emotional responses.
3. **Ego deconditioning** — detachment from the ego; described as probably the most difficult of the three.
