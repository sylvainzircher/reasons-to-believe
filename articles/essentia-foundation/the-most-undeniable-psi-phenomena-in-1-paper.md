# Interview Summary: Professor Etzel Cardeña on Parapsychology & Consciousness

> _Interview conducted at the Assencia Foundation, Porto, at a conference on near-death experiences and psi research._

---

## Who Is Etzel Cardeña?

The **Thorson Professor of Psychology at Lund University**, where he leads the Center for Research on Consciousness and Anomalous Psychology. He is a world-leading researcher in hypnosis, altered states of consciousness, and parapsychology (psi) meta-analyses.

---

## The 2018 Review Paper

Cardeña's landmark paper in _American Psychologist_ was a **"meta-meta-analysis"** — he gathered every major meta-analysis he could find across decades of psi research (around 12–13 in total) covering two broad areas:

- **ESP (Extra Sensory Perception / Anomalous Cognition)** — knowing things you shouldn't be able to know
- **Psychokinesis (PK)** — mentally affecting physical matter

His key finding: **every single meta-analysis** across every experimental method showed supportive evidence for psi. He argues that if only one or two supported it while others didn't, skepticism would be well-founded — but the consistency across all of them is striking. ESP showed stronger overall evidence than psychokinesis.

---

## Macro vs. Micro Psychokinesis

Cardeña distinguishes between:

- **Macro-PK** (visibly moving objects with the mind) — largely dismissed; the field is riddled with fraud and magicians, with almost no credible lab evidence
- **Micro-PK** (influencing random number generators or dice throws at a statistical level) — here there _is_ a small but reproducible effect. When someone tries to influence a machine outputting random 0s and 1s, the result shifts from exactly 50/50 to something like 50.5% vs 49.5% — tiny, but statistically significant across large samples

He offers a witty explanation for why the effect must be small: if people could freely influence matter with their minds, the world would be chaotic — and he jokes he'd have already been killed by the first person he ever failed.

---

## Why Aren't Psychics All Billionaires?

A common critique. Cardeña acknowledges it but notes that a consistent **30–32% accuracy** (vs. the chance baseline of 25%) is actually enormously valuable in real-world decisions — he suggests some high-net-worth individuals do quietly consult gifted psychics precisely for this edge.

---

## The Ganzfeld Effect

This is probably the most discussed finding in the interview. The **Ganzfeld** (German: _"whole field"_) is a sensory attenuation procedure:

1. The subject wears halved ping-pong balls over their eyes bathed in red or white light, and listens to white/pink noise — eliminating visual and auditory detail
2. After a relaxation induction, they are asked to mentally "tune in" to a randomly selected film clip that hasn't been chosen yet
3. After 30 minutes, they are shown four clips and asked to rank which one was chosen
4. By chance, correct identification should happen **25% of the time** (1 in 4)

**Result across hundreds of studies and thousands of participants:** people correctly identify the target roughly **32–33% of the time** — consistently, across labs worldwide. That's roughly 20% above chance. The person is still wrong two-thirds of the time, but the statistical anomaly is highly robust.

### A Vivid Example

A participant described _"boys playing on a meadow, bushes, a big balloon with big eyes"_ — the target clip turned out to be a Russian _Winnie the Pooh_ cartoon in which a bear floats up on a balloon toward a beehive while bees stare down at him. Elements bled into the participant's stream of consciousness without clear, literal perception — more like a waking dream than direct vision.

---

## Why Are Effect Sizes So Small?

Cardeña gives two main reasons:

1. **Evolutionary adequacy** — our minds are tuned for "good enough" survival (not bumping into walls, avoiding cars), not for psychic ability. There was no evolutionary pressure to develop strong psi
2. **Cultural suppression** — in Western society, these experiences are dismissed as witchcraft; there is no education or training in developing them, so they remain weak and underdeveloped

---

## Psi and Physics

Cardeña engages seriously with the philosophy of physics, referencing quantum mechanics interpretations suggesting that at a deep level, **space, time, and objects as we experience them may not be fundamental**. He argues that if physicists themselves cannot agree on what matter, time, and space actually _are_, then it is intellectually inconsistent to use "materialism" as a blanket reason to dismiss psi.

Psi phenomena, he suggests, point toward a view of reality in which apparent separation between people and events is not as absolute as ordinary experience suggests.

---

## The Problem With Altered States Terminology

A significant portion of the interview concerns Cardeña's taxonomic work on states of consciousness. Terms like _"trance," "hypnosis,"_ and even _"telepathy"_ are used so loosely as to be nearly meaningless — the word "trance" alone is applied to:

- Possession states
- Deep meditation
- Daydreaming
- Killing sprees

Together with 15 multidisciplinary experts, he developed a **taxonomy based on observable experiential alterations** (e.g., feeling outside one's body, vivid internal imagery, loss of conceptual thought) rather than defining states by their triggers. This allows meaningful comparison across induction methods and clarifies that hypnosis and psychedelics can produce identical experiential outcomes in some individuals.

---

## Ordinary Consciousness Problematized

Cardeña provocatively argues that science and culture place **"normal waking consciousness"** on a pedestal while pathologizing altered states — but that ordinary waking consciousness is responsible for war, ecological destruction, screen addiction, and social fragmentation. He suggests we should be at least as critical of our baseline state as we are of unusual ones.

---

## The Quality of Skeptical Critique

Cardeña is fairly scathing. He references the late parapsychologist **Chuck Honorton**, who documented that many skeptical critiques involved demonstrably false descriptions of experimental setups that were never corrected even when challenged. Other criticisms cited include:

- Claiming parapsychologists are just trying to "bring back religion" — without any survey evidence to support this
- Failing to actually examine the experiments being critiqued

He notes the quality of critique has generally **declined** over time rather than improved.

### On the File Drawer Effect

The concern that only positive studies get published is acknowledged, but Cardeña notes that statistical correction methods show you would need _hundreds_ of unpublished null-result studies to neutralize the published evidence — implausible given how few parapsychologists exist and how little funding the field receives.

---

## Jim Carrey and the Fluidity of Identity

The interview closes with a discussion of Jim Carrey's total immersion into Andy Kaufman during filming of _Man on the Moon_ (documented in _Jim & Andy_). Cardeña uses this as a springboard to discuss the **fluidity of identity**:

- We all become "someone else" every night in dreams
- Even in the hypnagogic state between waking and sleep, the sense of self can shift dramatically
- The "self" we experience as solid is, in his view, a **useful narrative fiction** maintained by habit, mirrors, and social routine — not a fixed metaphysical reality

> _"We are not this solid person. We are changing. We're shifting every day."_

---

## Personal Reflections

Cardeña does not consider himself psychic, but takes his own dreams seriously as potential signals — checking on people he dreams about in distress. He says his work in parapsychology has not made him hypervigilant or credulous, but has made life feel **more mysterious, meaningful, and open**:

> _"It just makes things even more extraordinary."_
