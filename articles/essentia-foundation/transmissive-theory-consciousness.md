## 1. The Transmissive Theory of Consciousness

William James proposed roughly 100 years ago that the brain may act as a **filter or receiver** of consciousness rather than its generator. Rouleau brings this philosophical idea into the laboratory.

**Key distinction:**
| Model | Brain's Role | Implication for Death |
|---|---|---|
| Productive | Generates consciousness from cellular activity | Consciousness ends at death |
| Transmissive | Filters/receives consciousness from outside | Consciousness may survive death |

> Rouleau's important nuance: **productive and transmissive functions are not mutually exclusive.** The brain can do both simultaneously.

The radio analogy: if you smash a radio, the broadcast signal still exists. The radio was never creating the music — it was receiving it. Reassembling the radio doesn't recreate the signal; the signal was always there.

## 2. Electromagnetic Fields and the Brain

Over a century of research links electromagnetic fields to brain function across species.

### In Non-Human Animals

- **Birds** navigate using Earth's magnetic field; solar coronal mass ejections (visible as the aurora borealis) disrupt their internal compasses entirely
- **Bumblebees** show altered swarming behaviour during geomagnetic disturbances
- **Fish** alter navigation patterns in response to electromagnetic perturbations

### In Humans

- Geomagnetic disturbances correlate with **increased psychiatric hospital admissions**
- Epileptic inpatients have **more seizures** during elevated geomagnetic activity — replicated across early and late 20th century datasets
- **REM sleep and dream content** change as a function of geomagnetic conditions and body orientation during sleep
- **Premonition-type dream experiences** cluster around periods of geomagnetic change

### Field Intensities (for context)

| Source                 | Intensity       |
| ---------------------- | --------------- |
| Clinical TMS           | ~1 Tesla        |
| MRI                    | ~1 Tesla        |
| Earth's magnetic field | ~50 microtesla  |
| Human brain emissions  | 1–100 nanotesla |

### Key Frequency

- Earth's magnetic field oscillates at approximately **7.83 Hz** — extremely low frequency, well within the brain's own rhythmic range

## 3. The God Helmet — Dr. Michael Persinger

Rouleau trained under Dr. Michael Persinger, inventor of the "God Helmet."

**How it works:**

- A football helmet fitted with four solenoids (metal wrapped in wire)
- Running current generates a magnetic field aimed at the **temporal lobes**
- Fields used: low intensity (~10–50 microtesla), low frequency (under 100 Hz)
- The temporal pattern was based on actual burst-firing brain signals reapplied to the brain

**What subjects reported:**

- A **"sensed presence"** — the feeling of someone else in the room
- Usually a deceased person they had known
- The identity attributed to the presence was **culturally coded** (Christians reported God or Mary; Hindus reported Shiva or Krishna)
- The presence was consistently felt on the **same side of the body**

**Persinger's interpretation:** This replicates naturally occurring phenomena. Temporal lobe sensitivity to electromagnetic fields likely explains historical mystical experiences, prophetic visions, and phenomena associated with **temporal lobe epilepsy** (Geschwind syndrome — hyperreligiosity, compulsive writing, sense of being chosen). Historical figures such as Joan of Arc may have had this condition.

## 4. Postmortem Brain Tissue Research

To isolate the transmissive question from the confounding variable of _life itself_, Rouleau studied **fixed, postmortem human brain tissue** — structurally preserved in formaldehyde but physiologically inert (no metabolism, no action potentials, no gap junction activity).

### What Was Done

- Tissue was exposed to controlled electromagnetic fields in the lab
- Microvolt fluctuations were measured across different brain regions
- Spectrograms broke down the full frequency spectrum of voltage fluctuations

### What Was Found

1. Different brain regions **still filtered electromagnetic signals differently** — showing regional specificity just as a living brain would, despite having no active physiology
2. Retrospective analysis showed the tissue's baseline spectral activity was **measurably different on days of high geomagnetic activity** versus quiet days — and within the frequency ranges expected for those regions in a _living_ brain

### Significance

This is a **materials science result**: the brain's physical structure alone has transmissive and filtering properties, independent of biological function.

### Ethical Dimension

Rouleau and Persinger seriously discussed whether injecting current into postmortem tissue could influence a conscious percept in the deceased. Their conclusion: probably not, given the chemical changes from fixation — but the question is worth taking seriously, particularly under non-materialist metaphysical assumptions.

## 5. Michael Levin and Bioelectricity

Rouleau collaborates with **Michael Levin** (Tufts University) on bioelectricity and developmental biology.

### Key Levin Findings

- Cells use **electrical signals — not just genes** — to coordinate morphogenesis
- Disrupting electrical connections (gap junctions) between _healthy, genetically normal_ cells can **cause tumours**
- Restoring those electrical connections **reverses the tumours**
- Organisms (worms, tadpoles, rodents) raised in environments **shielded from external electromagnetic fields develop morphologically differently**

### Historical Precedents

- **Harold Saxon Burr** (late 19th/early 20th century): described a "blueprint for immortality" via bioelectric voltage distributions; predicted pregnancy from abdominal voltage readings
- **Rupert Sheldrake**: morphogenetic field concept — an external space containing the forms of living matter that cells draw on during development

### Implication

Biological form may be partly **instructed by external electromagnetic fields** — placing instructive information _outside_ the genetic system entirely.

## 6. Mind Blindness and Widespread Cognition

Rouleau and Levin co-authored work arguing we currently detect roughly **1% of the minds that exist**, because our intuitions about mind are locked to nervous-system-centric, human-like systems.

### Substrate-Independent Cognition

Cognitive properties like learning, memory, and problem-solving have been demonstrated in:

- **Plants** — anticipating sunrise, game-theoretic resource competition with neighbours, operating on different time scales
- **Single cells** — learning and problem-solving
- **Iron bars** — conditioned responses
- **Silver nanowire networks** — self-reorganising into efficient configurations when current is run through them
- **Machines** — explicitly called "machine learning"

### Escaping Mind Blindness

Requires theories of consciousness that don't presuppose neural substrates. Rouleau favours:

- **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** — uses the metric Φ (phi) to quantify integrated information in any system
- **Free Energy Principle / Active Inference** — applicable to any system with feedback loops

**Caveat:** Rouleau notes both theories quietly rely on prior validation against systems we already _knew_ were conscious through first-person reports — a bottleneck that cannot be fully overcome.

## 7. The SOUL Lab

**Self-Organizing Units Lab** — a deliberate nod to the soul, aiming to open up what was once placed in that black box.

### Approach

- Uses **bioengineering techniques** to build brain tissue constructs from scratch
- Varies shape, density, layering, and connectivity
- Watches cognitive capacity emerge as elements are added (Braitenberg's "uphill analysis, downhill invention")

### Key Finding

**Embodiment is essential.** Systems that cannot interact with an external environment are very unlikely to be conscious. Feedback from the world is a prerequisite for mind.

### On LLMs and Embodiment

Rouleau considers LLM embodiment an open question:

- The monitor and keyboard constitute a minimal form of embodiment
- But it's unclear whether the feedback is sufficient for adaptive response, or whether the system is simply executing prior training — "communicating with a ghost"
- Architecture and hardware likely matter

## 8. Death, Dying and Transition States

### Challenging the "Moment of Death"

Rouleau argues the modern medical concept of a discrete moment of death is simply inaccurate:

- Brain death unfolds **over minutes to hours**, inside later than outside
- EEG-based brain death criteria (very low amplitude synchrony) don't capture neurons that are still firing randomly but no longer synchronously
- The brain continues cellular communication for minutes to hours after clinical death declaration

### Near-Death Experiences — A Cautious Assessment

Rouleau regards NDEs as **experiences of the living near death**, not evidence of an afterlife:

- They can be reproduced in healthy people via brain stimulation or psychedelic drugs
- If an experience indistinguishable from an NDE can be had while fully alive, it cannot serve as evidence of post-death consciousness
- They are, however, evidence of interesting **transition states** in the dying brain

> "You want death experiences — and we cannot get those."

### What He Considers Better Evidence

Not reports of experience, but the **fundamental question of what the brain is doing** — productive vs. transmissive. Demonstrating transmissive function experimentally is both replicable and mechanistically grounded.

## 9. Earth's Magnetic Field as a Storage Medium

Persinger proposed that Earth's magnetic field could store all conscious experience, based on **energy equivalence calculations**:

- Energy of one action potential: ~10⁻²⁰ joules
- × average neuron firing rate (~10 Hz)
- × number of neurons in a brain
- × number of brains on Earth and throughout history
- = an estimate of total energetic "weight" of human thought and memory

This energy is within the storage capacity of Earth's magnetic field in terms of pure energy equivalence.

### What Has Not Been Demonstrated

- The **stability mechanism** — whether electromagnetically encoded experiences remain stable enough to be retrieved or transmitted to another brain
- Rouleau notes information may instead be stored **locally in matter** (synaptic patterns) while remaining in resonance with the broader electromagnetic environment

### A Possible Mechanism for Shared Phenomena

If brains influence Earth's magnetic field, and Earth's magnetic field influences brains, this creates a **common medium** that could account for:

- Shared dreams
- The "zeitgeist" phenomenon
- Crisis apparitions (e.g., Hans Berger's founding motivation for inventing EEG — his sister's simultaneous distress at the moment of his near-fatal accident)

## 10. Quantum Connections

### The Radical Pair Hypothesis

- **Cryptochrome molecules** (found in bird eyes and throughout the human brain, including the suprachiasmatic nucleus) have unpaired electrons
- The orientation of a magnetic field influences how these molecules break down — the products of the reaction are proportional to the field's orientation
- This provides a **quantum-level electromagnetic compass mechanism** likely governing bird navigation
- Human brains express cryptochrome too — suggesting we may transduce environmental fields by the same mechanism

### Internal and External Fields

The brain both **emits** electromagnetic fields (via neuronal activity) and **receives** them from the environment. Both streams can influence cryptochrome molecules — creating an interplay between internal biology and external electromagnetic environment.

## 11. The Hard Problem and Metaphysics

Rouleau views the hard problem of consciousness — how quantitative physical processes give rise to qualitative experience — as likely intractable from a purely objective standpoint.

> "You can never observe a black hole directly. You infer it from how light is lensed by its gravitational well. Subjectivity is similar — you can never really touch it from the objective point of view. All you can do is feel around the edges."

### On Idealism vs. Materialism

Rouleau does not see them as incompatible:

- Like a video game with an internally consistent physics engine operating within a broader designed system
- The material world can be treated as fully real and measurable _within_ an idealist framework
- Science reaches its limits at the boundary of the measurable; outside that, philosophy and mathematics must take over

## 12. Closing Reflections

Rouleau closes with a meditation on **memory as a window into the self/world boundary dissolving:**

- A memory is a physical trace of the world copied imperfectly into your brain
- You _physically alter the world simply by existing in it_
- The world copies itself into you; you leave traces in the world

> "Nature is continuous fundamentally. Language has discrete labels to describe a world. But nature doesn't work that way."

Combined with the transmissive model, this points toward a **different kind of immortality**: conscious experience leaving electromagnetic traces that may persist beyond an individual life — not as a ghost or soul in the traditional sense, but as patterns in the fabric of the physical world, potentially accessible to other minds.
