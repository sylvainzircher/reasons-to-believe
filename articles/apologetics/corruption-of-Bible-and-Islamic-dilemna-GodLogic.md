## Main Topics Debated

### 1. Biblical Inerrancy

### 3. Tahrif — Was the Bible Corrupted?

Both sides cited Islamic scholars but reached opposite conclusions.

**Two types of Tahrif discussed:**

- **Tahrif al-nas** — Textual corruption (actual words changed)
- **Tahrif al-ma'na** — Interpretive corruption (misrepresentation of meaning)

| Scholar                | Position Cited                                                                          |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Bukhari                | "None can corrupt the text... they only corrupted it by misinterpreting it."            |
| Ibn Abbas (in Bukhari) | People "wrote something with their own hands" — cited as evidence of textual corruption |
| Ibn Kathir             | Verse refers to Muhammad being mentioned in previous scriptures                         |
| Al-Razi                | Agreed corruption was interpretive only                                                 |

**Daniel's claim:** The majority of scholars believe in both types of tahrif.
**Avery's claim:** The most authoritative scholars — Bukhari, Ibn Abbas, Al-Razi — rejected textual corruption.

## Key Arguments For Biblical Integrity

### From the Quran Itself

- Quran 6:115 says none can change Allah's words — if the Bible is Allah's word, it cannot be corrupted
- Quran 10:94 directs Muhammad to consult previous scriptures — pointing to a corrupted source would be incoherent

### From Islamic Scholarship

- Bukhari explicitly states textual corruption did not occur
- Ibn Abbas and Al-Razi agree corruption was interpretive only
- These are among the most authoritative voices in Islamic tradition

### From Manuscript Evidence

- Thousands of biblical manuscripts predate Islam and are remarkably consistent
- No central authority existed with power to globally alter all manuscripts
- Jews and Christians were separate communities who would never cooperate on alterations

### The Logical Timeline Problem

- If corrupted before Muhammad — why does the Quran tell him to consult it?
- If corrupted after Muhammad — manuscript evidence dating before Islam contradicts this
- Either way the corruption narrative is logically inconsistent
