## Genesis & Archaeological Evidence

### Creation & Flood Accounts

Ancient Mesopotamian clay tablets record creation and flood narratives with striking parallels to Genesis:

- Pre-flood humanity angering God/gods
- One man warned to build a boat with specific dimensions, sealed with bitumen
- Animals boarding two by two
- A great flood wiping out humanity
- The boat landing on a mountain
- Sacrifices offered in thanksgiving

Earlier tablets tend toward monotheism, with polytheism increasing over time — suggesting theological drift from an original account.

### Tower of Babel & Table of Nations

- A Sumerian text appears to describe the Babel event
- The Table of Nations in Genesis is widely considered by historians to reflect the real **Uruk expansion** — a civilizational explosion out of southern Mesopotamia
- Nations bearing names matching Genesis appear in their respective regions at the right times

### The Patriarchal Period

Social customs, covenant protocols, personal names, and place names in the Abraham/Isaac/Jacob narratives are all specific to that era and geographic region, lending strong contextual credibility.

## The Five Cities of the Plain

### Zoar

The best-documented city — the **one city not destroyed**. Excavations (1980s–2000s) confirmed continuous occupation from before Abraham's time through Byzantine and medieval periods, matching biblical references in Isaiah, Jeremiah, Kings, and Roman-era documents. The **Madaba Map** (~542 AD) also marks its location.

### Cave of Lot

Excavated nearby; a Byzantine commemorative church with **three inscriptions mentioning Lot** was found in front of the cave. Pottery inside dates to the Early and Middle Bronze Ages, stopping abruptly around the time of the destruction — then unused for millennia.

### Sodom & Gomorrah

| City     | Likely Site  | Evidence                           |
| -------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| Sodom    | Bab edh-Dhra | Destruction by fire, never rebuilt |
| Gomorrah | Numeira      | Destruction by fire, never rebuilt |

Both occupied through the Early Bronze Age, both showing extensive fire destruction, and **neither ever rebuilt** — matching prophetic and gospel descriptions.

### Admah & Zeboiim

Tentatively identified as **Fifa and Khanazir** — unexcavated but surveyed. Same hilltop fortress layout, adjacent riverbeds (now dry) that Genesis describes as well-watered.

## The Exodus

### The Dating Debate

| View               | Date                 | Basis                                                                     |
| ------------------ | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Late Exodus        | 13th–11th century BC | Figurative/symbolic reading of biblical numbers                           |
| Early Exodus       | ~1446 BC             | Literal reading of 1 Kings 6:1 (480 years before Solomon's temple)        |
| Revised Chronology | Varies               | Radical revision of Egyptian chronology — rejected by nearly all scholars |

Dr. Kennedy supports the **early date (1446 BC)**. The 480-year figure in 1 Kings 6:1 is corroborated by:

1. Chronicles genealogy (~19 generations × 25 years ≈ 475 years)
2. Jephthah's statement in Judges 11:26 (~1100 BC + 300 years in Canaan = entry ~1400 BC)
3. Cumulative judges chronology, with overlapping judgeships accounting for the apparent excess
4. Parallel ancient Near Eastern temple dedication texts (Assyrian, Babylonian, Egyptian, Phoenician, Roman) that use real elapsed years in the same formula

### Amenhotep II as the Exodus Pharaoh

At least 12 lines of evidence point to Amenhotep II, highlights including:

- **Not the firstborn** — his older brother died earlier; he survived the plagues as the Bible describes
- **His firstborn son Webensenu died young** — the boy's mummy (~8–10 years old) was found in his father's royal tomb, highly unusual
- **Predecessor Thutmose III reigned 54 years** — matching the 40+ years Moses spent in exile
- **Exodus date (~1446 BC)** falls early in Amenhotep II's reign
- **Post-Exodus slave raid into Canaan** — claiming 100,000+ slaves and replenished chariots/weapons, matching the loss of Israel's labor force and the Reed Sea chariot destruction
- **Military campaigns largely ceased** for ~a century after him
- **Thutmose IV's Sphinx Dream Stele** — his successor constructed a divine justification for becoming Pharaoh rather than acknowledging his older brother's unexplained death
- **Manetho** (Egyptian priest-historian, 4th–3rd century BC) names the Exodus king as "Amen" — the Greek rendering of _Amenhotep_

## New Testament & Jesus

### Historical Existence

Virtually all ancient historians — including atheists and agnostics — accept Jesus as a historical figure. First and second century sources confirm:

- **Josephus, Tacitus, Suetonius, Celsus**: birth in Judea, Joseph as carpenter, disciples, miraculous activity, trial under Pontius Pilate during Tiberius's reign, crucifixion, death, and a reported resurrection

### The Empty Tomb

- Located inside the **Church of the Holy Sepulchre**, Jerusalem
- A first-century tomb matching gospel descriptions, continuously venerated since 33 AD
- Emperor **Hadrian** built a pagan temple over it to suppress Christian memory; Constantine later removed it
- Romans never produced Jesus's body; their only counter-narrative was to bribe soldiers to claim the disciples stole it

### The Nazareth Inscription

A Greek imperial edict inscribed on stone makes removing a body from a sealed tomb **punishable by death** — understood as a Roman attempt to legally suppress any repeat of the resurrection claim. Indirect archaeological evidence that the empty tomb was a known and troubling fact to Roman authorities.

## The Tel Dan Inscription & King David

### Discovery (1993)

During excavations at **Tel Dan** (ancient city of Dan, north of the Sea of Galilee), an Aramaic victory stele from a 9th century BC Aramean king was uncovered containing the phrase **"house of David."**

### Why It Was Transformative

- Came from an _enemy_ of Israel — eliminating any possibility of Israelite propaganda
- Confirms David as the **founder of a royal dynasty**
- Matches the Old Testament phrase "house of David" used multiple times
- Rapidly shifted scholarly consensus from "David is mythological" to "David was a historical king"

### Cascade Effect

- The **Mesha Stele** re-examined and also found to reference the house of David
- **David's palace** in Jerusalem partially excavated
- Fortress towns across his kingdom's territory confirmed an extensive realm
- Scholarly debate shifted from _whether_ David existed to _how extensive_ his kingdom was
