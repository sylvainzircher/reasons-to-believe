## Biology and Genetics

### Chimp DNA

The 98% human-chimp similarity claim originated from cherry-picked shared DNA segments, not whole-genome comparison. Full-genome analysis shows at most ~85% identity — at least 450 million DNA letter differences. Basic genetic similarity across mammals (dogs, cats, horses, chimps) reflects shared biochemical needs, not common ancestry. Chimps lack human intellectual, creative, and linguistic capacities. The conclusion: humans and chimps were engineered separately.

### Cell Origins

The origin of the first cell is one of evolution's greatest unsolved problems. Cells require proteins to make proteins, using DNA and RNA simultaneously — a three-way interdependence that cannot arise stepwise. Warm-pond chemistry experiments have produced nothing close to functional cellular machinery; natural processes tend to destroy biological hardware rather than assemble it. Biological codes are more complex than computer codes. Just as computers require designers, the cell requires a Divine Engineer.

### Junk DNA

Evolutionary theory predicted most of the genome would be non-functional debris from random mutations. The 2001 Human Genome Project found only ~5% of obvious function; scientists labelled the rest "junk DNA." The ENCODE project (2012) found at least 80% of the human genome is functional — refuting the junk DNA prediction. Some evolutionists objected that these results must be wrong since they contradict Darwinism — the argument treats the theory as unfalsifiable.

### Wonderfully Made (Human Embryology)

A fetus survives nine months in a water environment no adult could tolerate. The baby's blood vessels are structurally different from adults to supply oxygen during development. Fetal hormones reshape the mother's body to meet the baby's needs. The placenta prioritises the baby's nutritional needs. In the final weeks, the baby releases hormones signalling readiness for birth. This all-or-nothing interdependency could not arise incrementally. Psalm 139:13–14 cited.

### Evolving Bacteria

A 20-year study of E. coli tracked 40,000 generations — the bacteria remained E. coli. A fruit-fly study ran 600 generations (~12,000 human years) — they remained fruit flies. Antibiotic resistance is mutation within a species, not evolution into a new one. Each creature reproduces after its kind, as Genesis states.

### Whale Evolution

Darwin speculated bears evolved into whales. The two key fossil "missing links" (Pakicetus and Rodhocetus) were initially reconstructed with whale features from incomplete skulls; later more complete specimens showed normal land-mammal legs. Museum illustrations still show invented whale tails on these fossils. Whale hip bones, once claimed as vestigial legs, are now known to be essential for reproduction. No confirmed transitional fossils; fully formed creature kinds from the start.

### Vestigial/Useless Body Parts

The appendix, gallbladder, tonsils, wisdom teeth, thymus gland, tailbone, and little toe were all once declared evolutionary leftovers. Each has since been found to serve important functions (e.g. the appendix in immune function). The same applies to "junk DNA." God does not create junk; apparent redundancy reflects backup design after the Fall.

### The Seeing Eye

The human eye is more sophisticated than any camera: cornea (70% of light focus), iris (adjustable aperture), lens (variable focal length), retina (light-to-nerve conversion), optic nerve, ocular muscles including a pulley-ligament system, and brain processing (including automatic image inversion correction). All components work in concert. The eye could not have arisen by chance; Proverbs 20:12 cited.

### Versatile Hands

Forearm muscles control fingers, freeing the palm. The brain plans finger movements three actions ahead; keystrokes can be as brief as 60 milliseconds apart. Multiple grip types can be used simultaneously. Psalm 139 cited.

### Throwing a Strike

Pitching a baseball involves the pre-motor cortex, motor cortex, and cerebellum working together, routing continuous signals through the spinal cord to muscles, integrating distance data from the eyes, and timing ball release to the millisecond. All of this happens in under a second. Cited as evidence of the complexity and intentional design of the human body.

### Muscle Man

Human grip generates ~100 pounds of force through an intricate interplay of bone, muscle, and tendon. Nothing in muscular mechanics is simple; all points to purposeful engineering.

### Echolocation

Dolphins and bats both use biological sonar (echolocation) — yet one lives in water, the other in air. Both have specialised cochleae detecting ultrasonic frequencies. Bats emit pulses at 100 kHz that drop to 30 kHz on return, and a middle-ear muscle contracts during transmission to protect hearing. Dolphins use burst-pulse communication for right-of-way among feeding animals. The precision and integration of these systems in two unrelated animals, in different media, points to a common Designer who custom-engineered each for its environment.

### Man's Best Friend (Dogs)

Darwin's finch-beak variation and stickleback fish adaptation are variation within a kind, not evolution across kinds. Variation showcases the Creator's built-in genetic diversity, not common ancestry.

### Humans Are Unique

## Palaeontology and Fossils

### The Fossil Record (Sudden Appearance and Stasis)

Stephen Gould (an evolutionist) acknowledged the fossil record shows two features: sudden appearance and stasis. Creatures show up fully formed with no gradual precursors; their basic form then stays the same. Examples: crinoids, mosasaurs, beard worms, horseshoe crabs, lobsters, trilobites. Darwin himself acknowledged the Cambrian problem and hoped future fossils would fill it. They have not.

### The Cambrian Explosion

Cambrian rock layers contain nearly all basic animal phyla (echinoderms, arthropods, chordates) with no transitional fossils in Precambrian layers below. Cambrian arthropods had advanced compound eyes with over 3,000 interconnected lenses, tuned to water's refractive index. Fossil fish in Cambrian rocks resemble modern lancelets and hagfish, with full internal skeletal structure. Evolutionists claim all this appeared in 5–10 million years — a geological instant — which contradicts gradual evolution.

### Dino Cells

In 1990, a T. rex skeleton from Montana was found with soft blood vessels and blood cells still intact inside the bones. Other soft-tissue dinosaur finds include squid with usable ink (2009), lizards with skin, and dinosaurs with "gooey blood vessels." Soft tissue can only persist for thousands of years. These finds are inconsistent with millions-of-years dates and consistent with recent creation and the Flood.

### Dino Tracks

Dinosaur tracks are found on every continent, often spanning vast regions — only a global flood with continent-wide mud flows could explain this. Tidal oscillations during the Flood briefly exposed the highest land areas, creating fresh mud for dinosaurs to walk on. A subsequent mud flow sealed and preserved the prints. No baby dinosaur tracks are found — most hatchlings would have been overwhelmed early in the Flood. Modern shorelines don't preserve fossil footprints (they disappear in days), but Flood-deposited mudstone does.

### Dinos and Humans

The word "dinosaur" was coined in 1841 by Richard Owen. The Book of Job describes Behemoth with a tail like a cedar tree — consistent with a sauropod. Dragon legends from China, Greece, Alexander the Great's army, and Marco Polo describe creatures consistent with dinosaurs. Chinese zodiac and cave art across continents show humans and dragon/dinosaur-like creatures together. Soft tissue preservation in dinosaur bones confirms they cannot be millions of years old. Dinosaurs and humans were created on the same day (Genesis 1).

### Jurassic Omelette

Fossilised sauropod eggs in Hunan Province, China contain unborn dinosaur bones and remnants of proteins. Scientists noted these were victims of catastrophic flooding. Lab analysis found the proteins could only have survived thousands of years — not 190 million. Consistent with a global catastrophic flood a few thousand years ago.

## Astronomy and Cosmology

### Big Bang Problems

Three problems with the Big Bang: (1) **Antimatter problem**: the Big Bang should have produced equal matter and antimatter, but the universe shows no sign of the expected antimatter. (2) **Horizon problem**: rapid early expansion should have left hot and cold spots in the universe, but temperatures are remarkably uniform in all directions. (3) **Mature galaxies problem**: distant galaxies should appear young and chaotic (light from them left when they were allegedly young), but they appear fully formed and complex. Also, the Big Bang requires billions of years and posits the sun forming before the Earth — contradicting Genesis.

### Our Young Universe

Three indicators of a young universe: (1) **Moon recession**: the moon moves ~1.5 inches away from Earth per year due to tidal forces; 4 billion years ago it would have been touching Earth. (2) **Comets**: comets lose material each orbit and couldn't survive billions of years. (3) **Spiral galaxy winding**: if galaxies were billions of years old, their inner regions would have rotated so many more times than outer regions that the spiral arms would be completely wound up — they are not. All consistent with a universe thousands, not billions, of years old.

### Goldilocks Planet

Earth is in 16 known planetary habitable zones simultaneously — the only known planet meeting all 16. Mars: thin, unbreathable CO₂ atmosphere; 60× more sulfur in soil than Earth. Venus: crushing atmosphere with sulfuric acid clouds. Mercury: too hot. Earth: right distance from the sun, right gravity, right atmosphere. Not too hot, not too cold — designed by a loving God.

### Blue Stars

Blue stars are massive, extremely luminous (200× brighter than our Sun) and burn through hydrogen so fast they cannot last billions of years — the most massive cannot last even 1 million years. Yet they exist throughout a universe supposedly 13.8 billion years old. Secular scientists appeal to ongoing star formation from gas cloud compression, but gas particles in clouds exert outward pressure that far exceeds the inward pull of gravity — no one has observed this process. Blue stars fit a young universe recently created.

### Night Sky

The Sun is over a million times larger than Earth; Betelgeuse is ~1,000 times the size of the Sun. Astronomers estimate 170+ billion galaxies in the observable universe, each uniquely designed. No two spiral galaxies are identical. The diversity and uniqueness of stars and galaxies reflects a Creator who delights in variety.
