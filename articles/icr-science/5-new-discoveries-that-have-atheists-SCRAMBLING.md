## Discovery 1: James Webb Space Telescope Findings

**Researcher:** Dr. Jake Hebert, Physicist

The James Webb Space Telescope has photographed what appear to be fully mature galaxies at extreme distances. Under standard cosmology, these would have existed only ~280 million years after the Big Bang — far too early for galaxies to have formed under mainstream models, which require hundreds of millions of years.

**Key point:** One physicist suggested simply doubling the age of the universe to resolve the contradiction. Hebert uses this to argue that mainstream age estimates are unreliable.

**Biblical connection:** Challenges the long-age assumptions underpinning the Big Bang timeline.

## Discovery 2: T. Rex Maturation Rate

**Researcher:** Dr. Jake Hebert, Physicist

A revised study using overlapping partial growth ring curves from T. Rex long bones (tibia/femur) suggests the animal took up to **40 years** to reach maturity — significantly longer than the previous estimate of 25 years. Similar analysis of sauropod dinosaurs like Apatosaurus suggested maturation took up to **100 years**.

**Key point:** Animals that mature slowly tend to live longer. This implies dinosaurs had very long lifespans.

**Biblical connection:** Aligns with Genesis accounts of pre-Flood humans living 800–900 years. If longevity mechanisms applied to people, they likely applied to animals in the same environment. Hebert also cites preserved soft tissue in dinosaur bones as evidence they lived thousands, not millions, of years ago.

## Discovery 3: Human-Chimp Genome Similarity

**Researcher:** Dr. Jeff Tompkins, Molecular Biologist

The widely cited 98–99% genetic similarity between humans and chimps was based on cherry-picked protein-coding regions, ignoring large dissimilar portions of the genome. Using full genome comparisons, Tompkins arrived at **~84% similarity** — a figure also independently reached by conventional geneticist Richard Buggs.

In spring 2025, a landmark _Nature_ study resequenced the chimpanzee genome end-to-end using advanced long-read DNA sequencing technology. The actual similarity figure — **84–85%** — was buried in 171 pages of supplemental material rather than highlighted in the main 17-page paper.

**Key point:** Evolutionary statistical models require close to 99% similarity to be mathematically viable. An 84% figure, Tompkins argues, breaks those models entirely.

**Biblical connection:** Supports the biblical account that humans were uniquely created in God's image, not descended from a common ancestor shared with apes.

## Discovery 4: Rapid Sedimentary Rock Formation

**Researcher:** Dr. Tim Clarey, Geologist & Director of Research at ICR

Conventional geology holds that sedimentary rock takes tens of thousands to millions of years to form. A 2025 study examined industrial iron-processing slag deposited along the Scotland/England coastline between 1856 and the 1950s. Researchers found that the full rock-forming cycle — transportation, rounding of particles, deposition, and cementation — occurred in **35 years or less**.

**Evidence:**

- A King George V coin dated **1934** was found cemented inside the rock
- An aluminum can tab (not manufactured before **1989**) was also found in the rock
- The angular slag became rounded within just a few hundred meters of transport

**Biblical connection:** Supports the idea that the global sedimentary rock record — seen consistently across all seven continents — could have been deposited rapidly during a year-long worldwide flood approximately 4,500 years ago.

## Discovery 5: Dinosaur Proteins Published in Mainstream Journal

**Researcher:** Dr. Brian Thomas, Paleontologist/Biochemist

Thomas and collaborators published a paper in _Analytical Chemistry_ documenting original **collagen protein sequences** recovered from an Edmontosaurus fossil, confirmed by five independent analytical techniques. The study also quantified the amount of hydroxyproline (a key collagen amino acid) present — a first for this field.

**Key point:** Collagen has a known decay rate governed by the second law of thermodynamics. Even under ideal conditions (cool temperatures, no bacteria, no radiation), collagen cannot survive beyond several hundred thousand years. It cannot last millions of years.

**Broader context:** 130 peer-reviewed papers now document similar biomaterial finds — including blood vessels, proteins, and soft tissue — across all continents, geological layers, and creature types.

**Response from mainstream scientists:** Tended toward dismissal ("bad science") rather than engagement with the methodology. Thomas noted this mirrors his own earlier reaction before examining the evidence personally.

**Biblical connection:** Original soft tissue suggests these fossils are thousands, not millions, of years old — consistent with a young earth and the fossil record being a product of Noah's Flood.
