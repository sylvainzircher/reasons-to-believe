# Why Is the Universe the Way It Is? — Fine-Tuning, Cosmology, and the Two-Creation Model

## Overview

This is a conference talk by **Hugh Ross** (astrophysicist and founder of Reasons to Believe), including a Q&A session. It covers the fine-tuning of the universe as evidence for God, the purposes behind the universe's apparent "waste" and decay, the two-creation model in Christianity, and the resurrection as the core of the Christian faith. Ross interacts with atheist objections throughout, drawing on his own experiences debating physicists and his book _Why the Universe Is the Way It Is_.

---

## 1. The Atheist Objection: The Universe Is a Wasteful, Hostile Place

Several prominent atheists and physicists have argued the universe's scale, hostility, and apparent waste argue against a loving Creator:

- **Stephen Hawking**: the other galaxies in our universe do not seem necessary for our existence.
- **Victor Stenger** (_God: The Failed Hypothesis_): if God created the universe for humanity, he wasted enormous amounts of matter, energy, space, and time. A God who relegated humanity to a tiny point in space and time cannot be said to have special love for us.
- **Neil deGrasse Tyson**: the universe appears to be "out to kill us."
- Stenger also argued: "Does God really need so much pain and suffering to achieve his ends? This is a possible God, but a hideous one."

Ross's response: these objections all assume God had **only one purpose** in creating the universe — making a home for humans. Ross argues there are at least **12 known purposes** for why God designed the universe the way he did, and the challenge is to design a universe that optimally fulfils all 12 simultaneously.

## 2. The Fine-Tuning of the Universe's Mass

The universe's mass determines its elemental composition. When hydrogen is fused into helium in the first moments after the Big Bang, the universe passes through the relevant temperature window for approximately **20 seconds**.

- **Too little mass**: the universe spends only 18–19 seconds in the temperature window — too little helium is produced, and future stars cannot manufacture elements heavier than helium. Result: a periodic table consisting only of hydrogen and helium. No carbon, oxygen, nitrogen, or phosphorus — no possibility for life.
- **Too much mass**: the universe spends 21+ seconds in the window — so much helium is produced that stars rapidly convert all hydrogen and helium into elements heavier than manganese. Result: a periodic table lacking the lighter elements essential for life. No carbon, oxygen, nitrogen, or phosphorus.

The universe must be **precisely the mass it is** — to within **one part in a quadrillion quadrillion quadrillion quadrillion** — to produce the periodic table we have.

Of the 94 naturally occurring elements, **22 are "vital poisons"** — elements like arsenic where too little kills you and too much also kills you. Earth is the only known body in the universe where all 22 are at exactly the right levels for life. Nearly every element on the periodic table is anomalous on Earth relative to the rest of the rocky bodies in the universe (e.g. 60 times less sulfur, 90 times more titanium and aluminium) — but anomalous in precisely the right proportions for advanced life.

The universe also needs to expand at exactly the right rate:

- Slightly less massive → gravity can never collect gas into stars, planets, or galaxies. The universe would remain 100% diffused gas forever.
- Slightly more massive → gravity collapses everything into black holes and neutron stars (2 billion tons per teaspoon). Molecules, atoms, and life are impossible.

The fine-tuning of the cosmic mass density was initially estimated at **one part in 10^60**. The discovery of dark energy was thought to reduce this requirement, but instead **enhanced** it: dark energy must be fine-tuned to **one part in 10^122** — exceeding the best example of human engineering design by a factor of 10^97.

Ross states as a general principle: **every new discovery about the universe has enhanced the fine-tuning evidence, never decreased it.**

## 3. Why the Universe Had to Be Nearly 14 Billion Years Old Before Humans Appeared

The quantum physicist on the plane asked why God waited nearly 14 billion years before placing humans on the cosmic scene. Ross offers multiple reasons; the two highlighted here are:

### Reason 1 — Observational Completeness

At 13.79 billion years after the Big Bang, humans are positioned at the **only moment in cosmic history** when they can observe 100% of the universe's history:

- Any earlier (e.g. 5 billion years earlier): the light from the earliest events would not yet have had time to travel to our telescopes. We would see only the last 70% of cosmic history.
- Any later: dark energy would be accelerating the farthest reaches of the universe beyond the speed of light, making the cosmic creation event unobservable.

This is not merely a matter of timing — we also live at the **only location** in a universe of two trillion galaxies from which all of cosmic history can be observed (see Section 4).

### Reason 2 — Uranium and Thorium Abundance

For a planet to have a magnetic field that shields life from solar and cosmic radiation, it needs to be endowed with uranium and thorium. These radiometric elements:

- Produce heat as they decay, keeping Earth's iron core liquid → enabling the magnetic field.
- Provide energy for plate tectonics → enabling oceans and continents.

Uranium and thorium abundance in the universe reached its peak **when the universe was slightly more than 9 billion years old** — i.e. approximately **4.5 billion years ago**. Earth formed at **4.5662 billion years ago** — at the precise peak of uranium/thorium abundance.

### Reason 3 — Stellar Stability

Stars are unstable when young and when old; they are maximally stable in middle age. Our sun has been burning for 4.5 billion years, but only approximately **50,000 years ago** did its luminosity stabilise sufficiently and its flaring activity decrease enough to permit global human civilisation. When the sun was young, its flaring activity was **100,000 times greater** than today (note: the axis on this graph is logarithmic).

The optimal timing = 9.2 billion years (universal age at Earth's formation) + ~4.7 billion years (Earth's age at the emergence of stable civilisation) = ~13.9 billion years.

## 4. Why the Universe Must Be So Dark — and Why We Live in the Darkest Part

The universe is **99.73% dark stuff** (dark energy + exotic dark matter + ordinary dark matter). The visible matter (stars, planets, galaxies, nebulae) makes up only **0.27%**.

All of this dark material must be exquisitely fine-tuned for life to be possible. Dark energy alone must be fine-tuned to one part in 10^122 — a precision that exceeds the best human engineering by 10^97 times. This implies the designer of dark energy is at minimum 10^97 times more intelligent and better-resourced than the most brilliant human scientists.

Crucially, life requires not just a dark universe overall, but placement in the **darkest inhabitable part of that universe**:

### The Laniakea Supercluster

There are tens of thousands of superclusters (clusters of clusters of galaxies). All but one — the **Laniakea Supercluster** we inhabit — are roughly basketball- or football-shaped, with galaxy clusters jammed tightly together forming glowing balls of light. Laniakea is unique: its clusters are stretched out along long filaments and subfilaments, resembling a praying mantis. Life is only possible along a subfilament; our local group sits at the **nexus of three subfilaments**, in the least dense part of the supercluster.

### The Local Group

Within Laniakea, our local group of galaxies is exceptional: it contains **no giant galaxies** (unlike all other groupings examined). It has only two large galaxies — the Milky Way and Andromeda — which are **far apart** (Andromeda is 2.5 million light years away), ensuring they don't block our view of the distant universe.

### The Milky Way Galaxy

Within the Milky Way, our solar system sits **halfway between the Perseus and Sagittarius spiral arms** — the darkest location in the Milky Way where life is possible — on the inner side of the **Local Arm**, in a region with a paucity of bright nearby stars and dense interstellar molecular clouds.

### The Local Bubble and Local Fluff

Our solar system sits inside the **Local Bubble** — a cavity blown out by supernova eruptions 10–12 million years ago — making the night sky dark enough to see the extreme reaches of the universe. Inside the Local Bubble is the **Local Fluff** (a region of slightly higher density), providing protection from deadly radiation while enabling extragalactic astronomy. Our sun entered the centre of the Local Fluff only **200,000 years ago** and will exit in approximately 100,000 years — placing us in the optimal window.

Ross: it might be a coincidence that we live at the one _time_ in cosmic history when all of it is observable. It cannot be a coincidence that we simultaneously live at the only _location_ in a universe of two trillion galaxies from which all of cosmic history can be observed. **"Someone wanted us to be able to read the book of nature."**

## 5. Why Does the Universe Have So Much Decay (Thermodynamics)?

The second law of thermodynamics — the pervasive tendency toward decay — seems at odds with a loving God. Romans 8:21–22 acknowledges the entire creation has been "groaning, subject to its bondage to decay."

Ross argues the level of thermodynamics (entropy/decay) in this universe is **optimally calibrated** for multiple purposes simultaneously:

### Decay Is Necessary for Physical Life

- Without a very high rate of decay, stars cannot form and cannot burn stably.
- Planets cannot form.
- Plants cannot produce food (photosynthesis and decomposition both require thermodynamic processes).
- Animals cannot digest food and convert it to energy.

### Decay Restrains Evil

God designed the laws of physics — gravity, electromagnetism, the strong and weak nuclear forces, and thermodynamics — specifically for the **permanent eradication of evil** and the **redemption of humanity**. Two principles operate:

**1. The decay rate is not so high as to discourage productive work.**
Every human achievement decays eventually, but slowly enough to motivate effort. A book may last 50 years; a garden needs attention every two weeks. The differential motivates different kinds of work.

**2. The decay rate is not so low as to let human sin go unrestrained.**
Genesis 3 shows God's response to sin: increased pain in childbirth and increased difficulty in work. The laws of physics did not change, but sinful management of the earth causes it not to produce as God intended. The principle: the more sin you commit, the more work, pain, and wasted time you experience in dealing with its consequences. Since humans are biologically averse to extra work, pain, and wasted time, **the laws of physics motivate virtue and discourage evil**.

### The Space-Time Dimensions Restrain Evil

- **Linear time** (single dimension, cannot be stopped or reversed) limits the number of human contacts and therefore limits the scale of damage any one person can do.
- **Space** separates people, further limiting harm.
- **Short lifespans**: God allowed people to live 800–969 years in early Genesis, but this tilted the advantage toward evil — allowing serial killers, tyrants, and corrupt rulers to operate for centuries. The population quickly became almost entirely evil (Genesis 6). A pre-flood murder rate of **over 95%** is calculated in Ross's book _Navigating Genesis_. God blessed humanity with shorter lifespans to restrain evil — illustrated by the point that Hitler lived only 56 years and Stalin 62 years, saving tens of millions of lives.

### Decay Prepares Humans for the New Creation

Using the analogy of his physics programme at the University of British Columbia (38 classroom hours per week, 4 years instead of 5), Ross argues that **optimised suffering prepares people** for what follows. God intends suffering to be calibrated — enough to produce growth, not so much as to cause people to give up.

## 6. The Two-Creation Model

Ross argues Christianity is unique among worldviews in being a **two-creation model**:

**Creation 1 (This Universe)**

- God creates and designs the universe and Earth for life (Genesis, Job, Psalm 104).
- God places Adam and Eve in Eden.
- God intentionally allows Satan — the most powerful and wisest creature ever created, and the first to sin — into the Garden to test humanity with the greatest possible evil.
- Adam and Eve choose to experience evil personally rather than trust God's instruction.
- The fallen world follows: evil, suffering, decay.
- God brings his redemptive offer to all people groups.
- When the full number of those God intends to redeem have been redeemed, God permanently eradicates evil and gathers those who want nothing to do with him into permanent separation from his creation.

**Creation 2 (The New Creation)**

- Redeemed humans are placed in the new creation.
- They rule with Christ over the angels.
- The angels, who can only _observe_ God's grace from outside, are intently watching humans experience it directly (1 Peter 1:12). This means redeemed humans will be equipped for roles and ministries that angels cannot fulfil.
- No death, decay, disease, or disability.
- No need for gravity, thermodynamics, or electromagnetism.
- No constraint to three spatial dimensions or a single time dimension.
- Relationship limits are removed: in the new creation, permanent intimate fellowship with every other being simultaneously becomes possible — which is why marriage will no longer be necessary (Matthew 22:30). Ross speculates God will release humans into "geometric time," allowing e.g. the Apostle Paul to have a billion simultaneous one-on-one conversations.
- 1 Corinthians 2:9: "No one can think or imagine how great and wonderful the rewards and joy and love will be in the new creation."

The fallacy in Lawrence Krauss's atheist paper in the _Astrophysical Journal_ (which concluded the universe offers no hope, purpose, or destiny) is the assumption that the universe is all there is. The two-creation model directly answers this: the universe was never intended to be permanent — it is a vehicle for the redemption of humanity and the permanent eradication of evil, leading to a new creation where the constraints of this one are no longer needed.

## 7. Q&A — Quantum Entanglement

A questioner asked about the 2022 Nobel Prize in Physics (quantum entanglement) and its implications for realism.

Ross's response:

- Quantum entanglement reflects the **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle**: at the quantum level there is irreducible uncertainty, and what appears to be "weird" non-local causality is really **hidden causality** — the causal mechanism is beyond the resolution of human observation, not absent.
- The weirdness disappears at larger ensemble scales.
- Analogy: spinning a coin on its edge — you don't know whether it will land heads or tails, but when you slam your hand down to stop it, you've interrupted the system. The causality was always there; you just couldn't see it beforehand.
- A South African theoretical physicist at a nutritionists' conference recognised that certain proteins in the human body operate like **transistors** — and they can only function if the quantum uncertainty is precisely calibrated. Too large or too small, and the proteins fail and all life dies. Even quantum uncertainty is fine-tuned.
- Brownian motion (statistical uncertainty at a non-quantum level) must also be fine-tuned.
- **"God does play dice, but he carefully designs the dice to get exactly the uncertainty he needs in order that physical life can exist."** (Response to the Einstein–Bohr debate.)

## 8. Q&A — Miracles

Ross was asked how miracles fit within a scientific apologetics framework.

His view:

- Personal experience of miracles is necessary for ongoing faith. The Israelites in the wilderness lost faith when they stopped experiencing miracles directly.
- 1 Peter 3:15 ("always be prepared to present good reasons for your faith and hope in Jesus Christ with gentleness, respect, and a clear conscience") is not merely an intellectual exercise — it is a promise: those who live this way will see God supernaturally bringing prepared people to them.
- 46 years of pastoral ministry confirm: those who practise 1 Peter 3:15 experience miracles regularly.
- 1 Peter 4 (hospitality) must be combined with 1 Peter 3:15 — hospitality includes listening, compassion, and serving others' physical, emotional, spiritual, and intellectual needs.
- Demeanour matters more than argument: "If you lose a debate but win the demeanour war, you've probably won the way."

On whether miracles ceased with the apostles (cessationism):

- The book of Acts simply stops — there is no closure statement suggesting miracles ended. An open ending implies an ongoing experience.
- The nine miraculous giftings in 1 Corinthians 12 are for **breaking down supernatural barriers to the spreading of the gospel**.
- Where there are no supernatural barriers (e.g. most of the modern United States), those particular gifts are not needed — preaching and teaching suffice.
- Where barriers exist (e.g. the Soviet Union under communism), Ross personally witnessed the gifts of 1 Corinthians 12 operating.
- Ross parts company with Pentecostals on this: "Those gifts come and go. They're there when the barriers are present. When they're not, God intends normative means."

## 9. Q&A — From Theism to Faith in Christ

A questioner asked how Ross bridges the gap from the scientific case for a creator to personal faith in Jesus.

- "The most powerful evidence for the Christian faith is the bodily resurrection of Jesus from the dead."
- The challenge is getting people to be _willing to listen_ to that evidence — which is why Ross first uses the cosmological/fine-tuning case.
- The key principle: give people the reasons _they need_, not the reasons you think are best. Ask questions first; find out what kind of unbeliever you're dealing with.
- Reference to **Greg Koukl**: "Ask questions."
- In the 19th century, three atheist lawyers put the bodily resurrection on trial and all became Christians. They concluded the historical evidence for the resurrection was stronger than the historical evidence that Napoleon was defeated at Waterloo.
- Gary Habermas is writing a 5,000-page compilation of historical evidence for the bodily resurrection.
- Evidence highlighted:
  - The disciples stealing the body: 11 untrained civilians overcoming a full Roman cohort (each soldier trained to defend 9 square metres against all odds) is not rational.
  - The Romans or Jewish leaders having the body: if so, they could have ended Christianity by producing it. They never did.
  - Within days of the resurrection, approximately half the population of Jerusalem became followers of Jesus — with the empty tomb visible to all.
  - Jesus was given a rich man's burial: 75–100 lbs of myrrh and aloe wrapped in linen, equivalent to a fiberglass cast ~3 inches thick, with a separate headpiece. John and Peter saw the **intact body cast with no opening** — and immediately believed. An empty, uncut burial cast is difficult to explain by any hypothesis other than resurrection.
- What would falsify Ross's Christian faith:
  - Proof that the universe had no beginning.
  - Proof that humans differ only in degree (not in kind) from other animals.
  - Proof that Jesus did not rise bodily from the dead.
- Debate with Peter Atkins in London: Atkins could not name a single discovery that would cause him to abandon atheism — even a physical appearance of God would be dismissed as delusion. Ross notes the asymmetry: as a Christian he was prepared to be falsified; Atkins was not.

## 10. Q&A — Interpretations of Genesis

A questioner asked about Genesis and whether other Christian views overlap with Ross's old-earth view.

Ross surveys the main positions:

| View                                              | Key Features                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Day-Age View (Hugh Ross / Reasons to Believe)** | Each creation day = a long epoch; the days are in chronological order matching the scientific record                                                                                                                    |
| **C. John Collins's View**                        | The days in Genesis are not defined in length; certainly longer than 24 hours; chronological order is maintained                                                                                                        |
| **John Lennox's Intermittent Day-Age View**       | 24-hour creation days, each followed by a long age; then another 24-hour day                                                                                                                                            |
| **Revelatory View**                               | Each "day" = 24 hours in which God reveals what he _will_ do; the actual events take hundreds of millions of years                                                                                                      |
| **Framework View** (Lee Irons etc.)               | Days form a literary triad (Days 1–3 create realms; Days 4–6 fill them); time spans are indeterminate; strict chronology is not required — though proponents acknowledge the universe was created first and humans last |

Ross's argument for chronology in Genesis 1:

- Sequential numbering of days ("day one," "day two," etc.) already signals chronology.
- After each day: "it was good" and "it was so" — four distinct Hebrew linguistic markers unique to Genesis 1 in the Old Testament, all signalling chronological sequence.
- Ross: "There's no getting around that it is describing an actual chronology of events."
