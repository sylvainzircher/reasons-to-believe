# Catholic Miracles: A Case for God's Existence

## Summary of _Pints with Aquinas_ Interview with Ethan (convert & Catholic apologist)

---

## Guest Background

The guest, Ethan, is a 24-year-old recent Catholic convert. He was raised in a loosely Baptist/non-denominational Protestant household with minimal theological formation. As a teenager he went through a "new atheist" phase, becoming a zealous atheist who engaged heavily in religious debate. Key influences during his atheist period included the philosophical problem of evil, the problem of divine hiddenness, and a general sense that the universe appeared purposeless.

His return to theism began in college through a non-hallucinatory religious experience described as an "insight." He then gravitated toward Christianity due to the compelling character of Jesus, before settling into a "liberal Christianity on his own terms." Recognising this position as intellectually untenable, he eventually discovered evidence surrounding the Shroud of Turin and Our Lady of Zeitoun, began investigating apostolic Christianity (including Coptic Orthodoxy), and was ultimately drawn to the Catholic Church. He completed OCIA and was baptised at the most recent Easter Vigil.

## Framework: What Is a Miracle?

Ethan distinguishes several categories:

- **Providential coincidences** — events that could have mundane explanations but may reflect God's hand; not "miracles" in his strict sense.
- **Miracles proper** — events that exceed the power of nature; naturalistically inexplicable signs performed by God.
- **Private miracles** — personal, individual graces; not requiring public assent.
- **Vindicatory miracles** — public, extraordinary signs performed by God to authenticate a revelation or divine legate. These are the focus of the interview.

He also distinguishes **public revelation** (ended with the death of the last Apostle; Catholics are bound in faith to accept it) from **private revelation** (e.g., Fatima, Lourdes; Catholics are not bound in divine faith, though they may be bound in conscience to give reasonable credence to credible human testimony).

## 1. Our Lady of Fatima (1917)

### Narrative

Three illiterate shepherd children in Fatima, Portugal received a series of six pre-announced Marian apparitions on the 13th of each month from May to October 1917:

- **Lucia dos Santos** (age 10) — the primary visionary; could see and hear the lady
- **Jacinta Marto** (age ~7) — could see and hear but did not speak to the lady
- **Francisco Marto** (age ~9) — could only see the lady departing; could not hear her

The apparitions featured perceptual asymmetries (each child experienced them differently), consistent behaviour enacted in real time before crowds, and transcripts of interrogations immediately following each apparition that corroborate the children's accounts. The local parish priest, to avoid accusations of orchestrating a conspiracy, kept himself at a distance and interviewed the children the day _after_ each apparition.

On **July 13th**, the lady promised: _"In three months I will make everyone believe"_ — a pre-announcement of a public miracle, made in response to Lucia's petition for a sign. This exchange is documented in contemporaneous transcripts, corroborated by independent witnesses who heard Lucia's side of the dialogue in real time.

In **August**, the local anti-clerical administrator abducted the children, threatened them with boiling water, claimed their companions had been killed, tried bribes and "police tricks" to force them to recant or reveal the secret — all to no effect. Our Lady reportedly kept her August 13th appointment anyway, manifesting to the crowd (without the children) through an explosion and atmospheric phenomena.

### The Miracle of the Sun — October 13th, 1917

**Setup:** 30,000–100,000 people gathered at the Cova da Iria in heavy rain. Crowds extended across the entire field, with some witnesses kilometres away using binoculars.

**The event:** At approximately solar noon, the rain abruptly stopped, clouds parted, and witnesses reported:

- The sun appeared as a **pale, moonlike disc** — painless and comfortable to gaze at directly, despite being at its zenith with no observable cloud cover.
- The disc appeared to **spin rapidly**, emitting sparks in a rotating pattern (described as "firework wheels").
- **Colour changes** swept across the scene in sequence — most consistently violet/purple and then gold/yellow — tinting the landscape, people's clothing, and faces.
- The sun appeared to **fall from the sky** toward the crowd on three occasions before receding, causing widespread panic.
- Witnesses reported a **strong sensation of warmth** and, in many cases, their completely soaked clothing dried rapidly.

**Key evidence:**

- **Ophthalmological impossibility:** Staring at the midday sun causes retinal damage, pain, and after-images. None were reported by the vast majority of witnesses. Photographs show people gazing upward with undilated pupils.
- **Photographic analysis (Fr. Dr. Felip Dur):** Using photogrammetric shadow-cue techniques (validated to within 1° of angular precision on control photographs), Fr. Dur demonstrated that the primary light source in photograph D115 was at a maximum angular elevation of ~28–32°, _not_ the sun's actual position of ~42°. This means an unknown, non-solar light source was present.
- **Drying pattern photograph:** A photograph of a boy's trousers shows a "cluder-shaped" wet/dry contrast pattern consistent with a pulse of directional infrared radiation that was partially blocked by his buttoned jacket — corroborating widespread testimony about sudden warmth and rapid drying.
- **Distant witnesses:** The phenomenon was witnessed over a radius of at least 40–50 km:
  - Witnesses in **Alburitel** (~15 km away) reported the same phenomenology — sun falling three times, colour sequences reflecting off the environment, spinning motion.
  - A witness in **Minde** (due south) saw the sun falling from the sky while looking _north_ (away from the sun's direction).
  - Witnesses in **Leiria** (~30–45 km away) reported a red flash above the mountains at noon — consistent with long-distance atmospheric scattering of multicoloured light from a local emitter.
- **Crowd psychology excluded:** Convergent perception across isolated groups spread over 50 km rules out mass suggestion or hysteria as an explanation.
- **Pre-announcement:** The event was publicly predicted three months in advance by illiterate children who had everything to lose if nothing happened (they had received death threats).

### Objections Addressed

**"Failed prophecy" about the war ending:** Documentary evidence — including Formigão's interrogation notes from October 13th itself — shows the prophecy was _conditional_: "If people say the rosary / do penance, then the war will end." A Portuguese professor at Harvard confirmed the conditional grammatical form. Since the conditions were not met, the prophecy was not falsified.

**Solar/naturalistic explanations:** Critic Scott Alexander's strategy of drawing analogies to other solar anomaly reports (Buddhist sun miracles, Benin City 2017, etc.) fails because: (a) those lack the physical evidence, distant witnesses, and pre-announcement of Fatima; (b) on a Catholic worldview, those events may themselves be real paranormal events, which only compounds the problem for naturalism.

### Message and Vindicatory Implications

- Our Lady identified herself as **Our Lady of the Rosary** and urged daily rosary recitation and penance.
- She requested the **consecration of Russia** to her Immaculate Heart, predicting that Russia would "spread its errors throughout the world" (communism) and forewarning of World War II.
- The secrets confirm: **hell** (vision of souls in torment), **the Immaculate Conception**, **the papacy**, and **Marian devotion**.
- Conversions to Catholicism were explicitly among the lady's petitions — framing the miracle as a vindication of Catholicism specifically.
- The incorruption of **Jacinta Marto's** body (exhumed intact in 1935 and 1951, despite Francisco's body fully decomposing) is noted as a further sign.

## 2. Padre Pio (1887–1968)

### Overview

Padre Pio (Francesco Forgione) was a Capuchin friar at San Giovanni Rotondo, Italy. He received the **stigmata** in September 1918 — wounds corresponding to the five holy wounds of Christ — which persisted for exactly 50 years until his death in 1968. He is notable as the most prolifically documented miracle worker in Church history, with a 108-volume _positio_ prepared for his canonisation.

### The Stigmata

**Origin:** While praying in ecstasy after Mass on September 20, 1918, Pio reported a vision of Christ crucified, who told him "I unite you to my passion." He emerged bleeding from wounds on his hands (front and back), feet, and side.

**Medical examinations — key findings:**

- **Fr. Benedetto** (first examiner): described the lesions as true perforations — "if you held writing up to one side, you could read it clearly through to the other."
- **Fr. Luigi Orione / Dr. Romanelli** (1918): palpated a void between the two thin membranes covering each wound; confirmed they were true anatomical lesions.
- **Dr. Agostino Gemelli / Dr. Bignami** (atheist physician, Rome): conceded they were real anatomical lesions not attributable to natural pathology; proposed (unconvincingly) that Pio had unconsciously completed naturally-forming necrotic lesions with iodine or carbolic acid. This theory was critiqued for failing to explain the absence of parallesional inflammation, the depth and morphology of the wounds, and the practical impossibility of maintaining them symmetrically for years.
- **Bignami's experiment:** Pio's hands were wrapped, his room searched for chemicals, and a three-person sworn committee monitored him. Result: wounds retained identical characteristics _and_ bled more profusely than before.
- **Dr. Giorgio Festa + Dr. Romanelli (joint visit):** Confirmed subcutaneous depth, absence of chemicals, no parallesional inflammation. Subcutaneous depth was later confirmed by the fact that Pio could not fully clench a fist.
- **Bishop Rosi (apostolic visitor, 1921):** Found _no visible breaking of the skin_ — yet bleeding continued through intact skin. Counterintuitively, Pio let the wounds appear to fully heal precisely when the most consequential examination of his life was being conducted, only for them to be confirmed again when Festa performed surprise hernia surgery in 1924 (Pio refused anaesthesia to prevent examination but passed out from pain).

**Anomalies of the stigmata across 50 years:**

- Wounds never became gangrenous, infected, or deteriorated despite open bleeding and sub-optimal treatment.
- Morphological characteristics remained stable in ways incompatible with any natural wound process.
- Bled abundantly throughout — estimated minimum of 250 ml/day; blood tests confirmed normal haemoglobin with _no iron-deficiency anaemia_, despite blood loss that should have been fatal within months.
- Wounds disappeared completely at death (September 23, 1968) with _no scarring_ — impossible for deep subcutaneous lesions, which always leave permanent scar tissue.

### The Odour of Sanctity

An intensely sweet floral perfume (variously described as violets, roses, and lilies) emanated from the wounds and blood, with properties incompatible with artificial perfume:

- Blood itself was fragrant — Dr. Romanelli consulted scientists who confirmed blood cannot naturally have a sweet odour.
- Intermittent: present at some times, absent at others, often perceived in "waves."
- Extremely strong: capable of filling entire rooms; some witnesses nearly fainted.
- Persistent: items that came into contact with Pio (bandages, gloves, hair cut years earlier) retained the scent for months or years.
- Selective: perceived by some people in a room but not others simultaneously.
- Pio himself claimed _never_ to have smelled it once in 50 years.
- Detected by people with no prior knowledge of the phenomenon — e.g., Dr. Festa's atheist colleague in his car, Dr. Festa's patients in his Rome office (neither knowing a blood-soaked cloth was present).
- Reported by a Lutheran seminarian in Philadelphia in 1978, 10 years after Pio's death, who independently described it to someone who had known Pio — with perfect correspondence.
- Bernard Ruffin (Lutheran biographer): "Without exception, those I interviewed who spent any time at all with Padre Pio experienced the aroma at some time or other."

### Extraordinary Fevers (Hyperthermia)

- Highest recorded human fever in history: **52°C (125.6°F)** — Padre Pio.
- Second highest: **50°C (122°F)** — also Padre Pio.
- Third highest: **48°C (118.4°F)** — also Padre Pio (on multiple occasions).
- By comparison, the previous Guinness World Record (46.5°C) involved a heat stroke victim found in a deep coma requiring 2 hours of aggressive ice-water treatment, who subsequently suffered multi-organ failure and generalised seizures.
- Pio during his fevers: remained lucid, conscious, and often mobile; recovered within days with minimal treatment; reported experiencing mystical "representations of the Lord" during them.
- Multiple physicians with separate thermometers — including the atheist Dr. Angelo Merlo — recorded these temperatures. Two thermometers shattered (exceeded maximum recordable temperature). Physical artefacts preserved in archives.
- Medical impossibility: at such temperatures, irreversible cardiac and neurological collapse should occur within minutes. Pio's brain and heart should have been destroyed.
- Pio himself confirmed in sworn deposition: "It's true. It happens sometimes when I am ill... Internal feelings, the contemplation or some representation of the Lord, like in a furnace — still always conscious."

### Healing Miracles (Selected)

**1. Giovani Savino (1949) — Eye regeneration**
A construction worker at Pio's convent had his right eye "completely annihilated" by a dynamite explosion. Medical records on admission confirmed total destruction of the eye. On February 25th — after Pio had predicted the accident, the patient had reported Pio's presence in the hospital (accompanied by the characteristic scent), and felt a slap on the right side of his face — surgeons discovered the right eye had fully regenerated. He retained good vision in it for the rest of his life while remaining blind in the left (damaged but not lost) eye. An atheist ophthalmologist converted on the spot. Historian Fr. Joseph Pius Martin (not known for credulity) researched all attributed miracles and concluded this was the most striking, declaring it "historically certain."

**2. Consiglia De Martino (beatification miracle, 1995)**
A ruptured thoracic duct produced a grapefruit-sized lump (estimated ~2 litres of lymphatic fluid) requiring urgent surgery. The day before the operation, she asked Padre Pio to "be my surgeon." Surgeons the following morning found the lump had completely vanished; chest X-ray and CT scan confirmed everything was normal. She was discharged without surgery or medication. A panel of medical experts declared the healing "extraordinary and scientifically inexplicable." Dr. Violi confirmed that spontaneous reabsorption of such a quantity of lymphatic fluid is impossible.

**3. Matteo Pio Colella (canonisation miracle, 2000)**
A seven-year-old boy admitted to the _Casa Sollievo della Sofferenza_ (the hospital founded by Pio) with fulminant meningitis deteriorated into septic shock with disseminated intravascular coagulation and simultaneous failure of nine organs. Doctors did not believe survival was possible and suggested halting resuscitation. His mother prayed prostrate on Pio's tomb and had a vision of Pio lifting her son to his feet. The child stabilised and improved within hours; after 10 days, the pharmacological coma was suspended and he awoke with no neurological deficits, asking for a Coca-Cola ice lolly and a PlayStation. Dr. Violi: "Survival of any patient [in this condition] has never been described; mortality is 100%." On waking, Matteo described an out-of-body vision of "an old man with a white beard and long brown dress" who gave him his hand and said he would recover — and identified him as Padre Pio from a photograph.

### Doctrinal Vindications from Padre Pio

Pio's life and private revelations directly confirm Catholic doctrines:

- **The Papacy** — prayed for the Pope as "his vicar on earth"; submitted obediently even when persecuted by Vatican officials
- **Sacramental Confession** — spent 12–15 hours daily in the confessional; demonstrated infused knowledge of penitents' unconfessed sins
- **Purgatory** — "I have seen so many [souls in purgatory] they don't scare me anymore"; reported souls visiting him on their way from purgatory to heaven to thank him for his Masses
- **The Saints in Heaven** — claimed supernatural knowledge of who was in paradise (e.g., Pope Pius XII); intercessory miracles occurred in his name after his death
- **Hell** — received a vision of hell and reported experiencing the suffering of the damned
- **Mary and the Rosary** — extraordinary Marian devotion; "The Madonna never refuses me anything I ask through the prayer of the rosary"; healed through Our Lady of Fatima's intercession in 1959
- **The Mass and Transubstantiation** — his stigmata bled most intensely during the consecration; he described suffering "the scourging from beginning to end of mass, more intensely after the consecration"

### The Carbolic Acid Objection

A note in Pio's handwriting requesting carbolic acid from an external woman was unearthed by historian Sergio Luzzatto. Response:

1. Even granting Pio had access to carbolic acid, it cannot explain the depth of the wounds, the absence of inflammation, the blood volume without anaemia, the odour of sanctity, the healings, or the post-mortem scarless healing.
2. The apostolic visitor Bishop Rosi already knew of the carbolic acid and found Pio's explanation (he was director of a minor seminary and needed it for sterilisation without a prescription) credible.
3. Dr. Festa found no chemical traces on the wounds and noted carbolic acid would _destroy_ perfume on contact.
4. Most tellingly: a master fraudster who had successfully deceived multiple physicians under strict surveillance for a year would not use a single, easily-traceable written note to a woman who immediately ran to the bishop as his procurement strategy.

## 3. The Eucharistic Miracle of Lanciano (8th century – present)

### Background

In 8th-century Lanciano, Italy, a doubting Basilian monk reportedly witnessed the host and wine transform perceptibly into flesh and blood during the consecration. The resulting relics have been continuously venerated at the Church of San Francesco in Lanciano ever since. A documented episcopal inspection in 1574 confirms the relics' ancient presence. The relic consists of:

- A circular piece of **flesh** (~55–60 mm diameter) with a concentric hollow cavity
- Five irregular **blood clots** preserved in a chalice

### The 1970s Scientific Investigation (Dr. Odoardo Linoli)

Dr. Odoardo Linoli, professor of anatomy and pathological histology (director of a regional hospital laboratory in Tuscany), was commissioned in the early 1970s to conduct a full forensic investigation. His atheist colleague Prof. Ruggero Bertelli of the University of Siena independently reviewed the histological preparations.

**Six key findings:**

1. **Macroscopic morphology:** The flesh is consistent in size, shape, and structure with a transverse cross-section of a human left ventricle — a fact never claimed by the Church or the tradition surrounding the relic.

2. **Histology:** Both Linoli and Bertelli independently concluded the tissue was striated myocardial muscle from the heart. A follow-up investigation in the 1980s confirmed additional features consistent with this diagnosis.

3. **Species identity (precipitin test):** A forensically validated precipitin test with bovine, rabbit, saline, and human controls produced a clear precipitin band exclusively with human serum — confirming human tissue "qualitatively unquestionable" (Dr. Serafini).

4. **Blood confirmation (thin-layer chromatography):** Linoli confirmed haemoglobin-derived compounds in the blood clots in triplicate runs, which he characterised as "fully satisfactory for the recognition of blood."

5. **Blood type:** Absorption-elution reactions on both the flesh and blood clots yielded concordant **AB** results — a blood type shared by all scientifically verified Eucharistic miracles and present in only ~4% of the modern Italian population.

6. **Protein electrophoresis:** Serum protein fractions (albumin, alpha/beta/gamma globulins) were present in proportions matching fresh human blood serum. This is anomalous to an extreme degree: the test requires orders of magnitude more preserved protein than is found in any ancient sample, and the normal gamma-band inflation seen in aged blood (used as a dating method) was absent.

**No preservatives or fixatives** were detected — yet the tissue has survived centuries in a porous, non-airtight reliquary in fluctuating humidity and temperature, without the biological scaffolding or chemical treatment necessary for any known preservation method.

### Why Fraud Is Excluded

- Heart muscle (myocardium) is among the hardest biological tissue to preserve due to high cellular moisture content.
- The forger would need to: (a) obtain a human corpse, (b) perform expert anatomical dissection, (c) take a transverse section of the left ventricle, (d) prepare intact coagulated blood clots, (e) apply _no_ preservatives, and (f) achieve centuries of preservation under non-ideal conditions — all while telling no one it was cardiac tissue.
- The "constant swapping" hypothesis requires an unbroken chain of independent, centuries-separated co-conspirators, each replicating the exact macroscopic and microscopic appearance, without being detected, while believing their actions condemn them to hell.
- Episcopal seals on the reliquary (intact from at least 1880) prevent substitution without traceable destruction.
- The protein electrophoresis results rule out any substitution close to the 1970s investigation, as the preservation state is inconsistent with recently prepared tissue kept under those conditions.

## Addressing Demonic Deception

The interview devotes substantial attention to the question of whether miracles could be demonic deceptions. Three categories of criteria for spiritual discernment are presented:

### 1. Theological Criteria

- Consistency with **preambles of faith** (what reason alone establishes about God).
- Consistency of all revelations with each other — since God cannot be a deceiver without undermining all knowledge.
- God will not permit demons to make an **invincible forgery of his signature** (i.e., a pre-announced miracle in God's name, manifested publicly, that leads people inescapably into error) — because this would destroy the possibility of revelation itself. Deuteronomy 13 and 18 explicitly treat predictive fulfilment of claimed prophecy as a discriminating criterion.

### 2. Moral Criteria

- Genuine divine legates bear **good moral fruit** in their own lives and in the lives of those who follow them.
- Our Lord's principle: "By their fruits you will know them" (Matthew 7) — bad trees cannot produce good fruit.
- Satan cannot sustainably drive out Satan (Mark 3); demonic deceptions will exhibit detectable moral rot in the seers or in the devotional effects.
- Contrasted: Fatima children (penance, accepted suffering and death, refused money, maintained integrity under torture) vs. Joseph Smith (documented polygamy under sworn denial, occult treasure-seeking, financial fraud).

### 3. Physical Criteria

- Certain physical effects exceed demonic power _in reality_ (though demons can simulate them in appearance): e.g., pre-announced miracles in God's name, regeneration of destroyed tissue, resurrection of the dead.
- God would not permit the physical criteria to be useless — since He relies on them (as did Jesus) to authenticate revelation.
- Exorcism cases (e.g., the "Julia" case documented by Dr. Richard Gallagher, board-certified psychiatrist) confirm that demonic phenomena, though sometimes extraordinary, have characteristic signatures distinguishable from divine miracles.

### Non-Catholic Miracles

- The speaker concedes miracles occur in non-Catholic contexts, including Our Lady of Zeitoun (Coptic Orthodox, 1968–1971) — seen by up to one million people including Muslims and atheists, with government investigation, healings, and no naturalistic explanation.
- However: (a) no credible miracle has ever vindicated doctrine contradicting Catholicism; (b) the overall _distribution_ of miracles — qualitatively and quantitatively — overwhelmingly favours Catholic contexts; (c) miracles in non-Catholic contexts are consistent with Catholic theology (God distributing charisms beyond visible Church unity; Zeitoun contains nothing antithetical to Catholicism); (d) the asymmetry itself is probative.

## Concluding Argument

The cumulative case presented:

- A naturalistically inexplicable atmospheric event (Fatima) predicted three months in advance, witnessed over 50 km, with photographic corroboration, by crowds including hostile and sceptical educated observers
- 50 years of medically documented, physically impossible stigmata on a man of extraordinary sanctity under strict surveillance
- Three healing miracles (one involving the regeneration of a destroyed eye; two with unanimous expert declarations of inexplicability)
- Fevers exceeding any recorded human survival by ~10°C, multiple times, with no lasting damage
- An ancient Eucharistic relic confirmed as human cardiac tissue of anomalous preservation, with no plausible fraud scenario

The speaker argues: "God exists. People just need to accommodate their worldview to that."
