# Is the Trinity Logical? A Rational Explanation

## Overview

This video addresses one of the most common intellectual objections to Christianity: that the doctrine of the Trinity is logically incoherent. The presenter argues that while the Trinity is beyond human comprehension, it is not illogical — and that higher-dimensional mathematics provides a useful analogy for how God could be three persons in one being without violating the laws of logic.

---

## 1. The Doctrine of the Trinity

The Christian doctrine of the Trinity holds three claims simultaneously:

1. There is one God.
2. God is three persons.
3. Each person is fully God.

---

## 2. The Standard Objection

Non-Christians typically argue that the Trinity is logically impossible. The objection runs:

- If there is one God, then only one person can be God.
- If each person is not the other, yet each is fully God, this is a contradiction.

To resolve this, critics suggest alternatives — but none of these are what Scripture teaches:

- **Partialism** — each person is one-third of God.
- **Modalism** — the three persons are merely different modes or personalities of one God.

The presenter rejects both as reductions of God to a level human minds can manage, rather than honest engagements with what Scripture actually claims.

---

## 3. The Key Principle: Incomprehensibility ≠ Illogicality

A central argument of the video is that **"I cannot fathom it" does not mean "it is logically impossible."**

The analogy used is **Euclidean geometry and higher dimensions**:

- Mathematics can describe four, five, or even eleven spatial dimensions.
- These are entirely logical and mathematically sound.
- Yet no human being can _visualise_ what a four-dimensional object actually looks like.
- The fact that we cannot picture it does not mean it doesn't or can't exist.

---

## 4. The Tesseract Analogy (Carl Sagan)

Carl Sagan's explanation of the **tesseract** (four-dimensional hypercube) is used as a key illustration:

- A line moved at right angles to itself becomes a square.
- A square moved at right angles to itself becomes a cube.
- A cube moved at right angles to itself (into a fourth dimension) would generate a tesseract.
- We cannot see or fully picture a tesseract, but we can see its **three-dimensional shadow** — two nested cubes with all vertices connected.
- In a true tesseract, all lines are equal and all angles are right angles — something its shadow cannot show us.

**The analogy to the Trinity:** Just as a two-dimensional being could not fathom a cube (a 3D object with six equal square faces), we as three-dimensional beings cannot fully fathom a being that exists on a higher plane of existence. But that does not make such a being illogical.

---

## 5. C.S. Lewis on Dimensions and the Trinity

C.S. Lewis is quoted to make the same point in theological terms:

- On the human level, one person = one being; two persons = two separate beings.
- This is analogous to a flat 2D plane where one square is one figure and two squares are always two separate figures.
- On the divine level, personalities exist but are **combined in new ways** that a being at our level cannot imagine.
- God is three persons while remaining one being — just as a cube is six squares while remaining one cube.
- This is not a contradiction; it is a **super-personal** reality beyond our dimensional experience.

---

## 6. God Is Greater in All Ways — Including Ontologically

The presenter makes a broader ontological argument:

| Human attribute      | God's greater equivalent |
| -------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power                | Omnipotence              |
| Knowledge            | Omniscience              |
| Moral capacity       | Moral perfection         |
| Unitarian personhood | Multi-personal being     |

The logic: if God is greater than humanity in every other attribute, it follows that his mode of existence is also greater — i.e. not limited to the Unitarian structure that applies to finite human persons. A Unitarian God would simply be a human-shaped deity scaled up in power, not a truly transcendent being.

---

## 7. Why Three Persons and Not More?

A natural follow-up question addressed: if multi-personality is a mark of God's greatness, why not four or ten persons?

- What makes God greater is the **capacity** to be multi-personal, not the specific number.
- God can be as many eternal persons as he eternally wills.
- Scripture reveals him as three co-eternal persons — for reasons only God fully knows.
- Three is not a ceiling imposed by logic; it is what God has revealed about himself.

---

## Conclusion

The Trinity is:

- **Not illogical** — it does not violate the laws of logic.
- **Unfathomable** — it exceeds the limits of human three-dimensional comprehension.
- **Ontologically fitting** — a trinitarian God better matches the description of a being greater than humanity in all respects than a unitarian God would.
- **Analogically accessible** — higher-dimensional mathematics (tesseracts, hypercubes) shows that logical realities can exist beyond our capacity to visualise them.
